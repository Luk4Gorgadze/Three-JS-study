Strawberries may look like ordinary berries, but botanically they are something entirely different. The juicy red part we eat is actually an enlarged flower receptacle, while the tiny specks on the surface are individual fruits called achenes. A single strawberry can carry around 200 of these miniature fruits, making it one of the few fruits that wears its seeds on the outside. Their unique structure, bright color, and sweet aroma have made strawberries one of the world's most popular fruits.
